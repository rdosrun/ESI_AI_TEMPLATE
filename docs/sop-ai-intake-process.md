# SOP: AI Use Case Intake Process

## Purpose

This SOP gives teams a consistent way to request, evaluate, and prioritize AI proof-of-concept work. The goal is to focus on business value, data readiness, risk, and measurable outcomes before building.

## Intake Steps

1. Submit the use case using the stakeholder discovery template.
2. Confirm the business owner and technical owner.
3. Identify the documents, systems, and data sources involved.
4. Review privacy, security, compliance, and vendor constraints.
5. Define KPIs and acceptance criteria.
6. Score the use case for feasibility, value, urgency, and risk.
7. Approve, defer, or reject the pilot.
8. If approved, create a proof-of-concept backlog and demo plan.

## Minimum Required Information

- Business problem statement.
- Target users and workflow.
- Data sources and data owner.
- Expected benefit.
- Known risks.
- KPI targets.
- Timeline and executive sponsor.

## Review Questions

- Is the business problem clear enough to measure?
- Is the data approved for this use?
- Can the AI workflow cite or explain its sources?
- What happens when the answer is uncertain?
- Does the workflow need human approval before action?
- Can the pilot be delivered without creating production risk?

## Decision Outcomes

| Outcome | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Approve | The pilot has a clear owner, data source, KPI, and manageable risk. |
| Defer | More discovery, data access, or policy review is needed. |
| Reject | The use case is not suitable for AI or does not meet business/risk standards. |

## Operating Cadence

- Intake review: weekly during active pilot intake.
- Pilot checkpoint: every two weeks.
- KPI review: monthly.
- Production readiness review: before expanding beyond the pilot group.
